# 💡 Define the problem before solving it

Most bad solutions are answers to the wrong question. Before generating options, spend time
stating the problem precisely: what's actually wrong, for whom, and how you'd know it was
fixed. A sharp problem statement narrows the solution space and prevents effort spent solving
symptoms instead of causes.

## Why it matters
- A vague problem invites a sprawling, unfocused search for solutions.
- Writing down "done looks like ___" gives you a test to check any solution against.
- Re-stating the problem often *is* the breakthrough — the fix becomes obvious.

## A quick checklist
- What is the observed problem, concretely?
- Whose problem is it, and why does it matter now?
- What would "solved" look like — measurably?
- What's the *root* cause vs. the symptom?
