# 🗒️ Notes (Zettelkasten)

> [!info] Atomic, evergreen, in your own words
> This is your thinking space. A **permanent note** captures **one idea**, written in full
> sentences as if explaining it to a future you, and **linked** to related ideas.
> This is where knowledge actually compounds.

## The two note types
- **[[Templates/Literature Note|Literature Note]]** — captures an idea *from a source* (book, article, talk) in your own words, with the citation.
- **[[Templates/Permanent Note|Permanent Note]]** — a refined, standalone idea connected into your web of notes.

## How to write a good permanent note
1. **Atomic** — one idea per note.
2. **Self-contained** — understandable without opening anything else.
3. **In your words** — never copy-paste; rephrase to force understanding.
4. **Linked** — connect it to at least one other note and a **[[Maps of Content/Maps of Content|MOC]]**.
5. **Titled as a claim** — e.g. "Spacing repetitions beats cramming", not "Memory".
